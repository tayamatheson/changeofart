My Personal Experience

Although I am a Workshop Coordinator at my volunteer position, I haven’t participated in many art workshops as I ’m somewhat uncomfortable in group learning environments that are outside the traditional institutions, I am comfortable with. Despite this, I was excited to experience an art workshop and learn something new – poetry. I’m quite anxious and stressed today, so I normally would not participate in something like this, I would often hide away in my room, read, draw or simply watching movies until I felt better. While I know art is a form of therapy for me, I am really unsure about how doing a group art activity will affect me at this time.

At the beginning, I felt awkward and shy – I didn’t want to participate, speak up, share my opinions or answer questions. I decided I was simply going to observes. However, after a while of completing the tasks and watching others share, I began to open up and feel more comfortable with sharing my own work. Especially after being in quarantine alone for so many months, it was nice to interact with people – most I had never met before – even over the internet.

Participating in an art workshop was not only a lot of fun, but it helped the way I was feeling. I felt less anxious. I felt less stressed. I felt more focused and happier. Even outside of COVID-19, mental health can be very difficult to manage, and hearing other people’s stories about their own troubles (whether it be mental health or not) through the creation of poetry was incredibly enlivening. I struggle to share the way I feel, often downplaying it or masking it, yet I felt comfortable sharing small pieces of it in that workshop. I felt like a small community was created in that hour and a half period, where we all knew something deeper about each other that perhaps not many people we knew well even knew about us. We shared our stories and listened to the traumas willing to be spoken. It didn’t eliminate my anxiety, but it felt like it was easier to breathe through it knowing that I was not the only one who was struggling in some way.
