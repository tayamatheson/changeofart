Taya Matheson

I am a 22-year-old arts management student studying in Melbourne, Victoria. As someone who has both participated in the act of creation and experienced ongoing anxiety at varying levels throughout my entire life, I've always been interested in how these two things intersect. Studying Art History and Curating as my undergraduate showed me how mental health was embedded into art through history, yet I felt there was a significant lack of research and experimentation into the ways they may interact. 

About the Project

This blog was developed in addition to my final project for my Master of Arts Management at RMIT University, Melbourne. The research project centered around the hypothesis that art and creativity have a positive impact on mental health - on both an individual and community level. The blog aims to document my personal experience with artistic and creative activities and their affect on my mental health. 

Aims of the Research Project

Asses the personal experiences showing the relationship between impaired mental health and participation in the arts.         

Present the startling facts on Australia's mental health and address the need for change.         

Add to the, in my opinion, limited research on the connection between arts participation and improved mental health experiences.
