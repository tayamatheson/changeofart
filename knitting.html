My Personal Experience

Today wasn’t a good day. I went to Kmart on my way home and bought knitting needles and yarn for the first time in a few years. I used to love knitting, but over the years, I had kind of forgotten about it, so while I’m not feeling good, I am excited to try it out again. I’m not expecting there to be a huge change in my mood, but I feel like it will make me slightly calmer.

I couldn’t remember how to cast on, so after I did some googling, I began knitting. At the beginning, it was somewhat frustrating. I thought I would go into the activity and just remember what I was doing, and because I was already anxious, I almost gave up. But I stuck it out and tried my best, and after a while, I did remember. It felt like a reminder that sometimes you have to give yourself time and understand that you require time and patience.

I was wrong. Knitting decreased my anxiety so much. Before, I was antsy and overthinking, now, I’m calm and feel like I can think properly. I felt happy because I could still do this skill I learnt 10+ years ago and that I created something I’ve been wanting to for so long. It’s so simple, but the act of creating something useful (I created a coaster) is powerful. You are using your hands, your mind, your talent and producing something that will not only be looked at, but utilised.
