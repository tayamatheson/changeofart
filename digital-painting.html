My Personal Experience

Today is a very anxious and stressful day. I do not feel like pulling out my iPad and Apple Pencil and creating something. However, digital painting is my main form of creation, and it is my way of blocking out the world and solely focusing on creation – no overthinking or reacting to my anxieties. Despite this, it is not often I will paint digitally when my anxiety is at the height it is today.

For myself, it is common knowledge that digital painting helps regulate my emotions and provide needed time to express myself through creativity without judgement. Almost immediately I felt as if my anxiety was washing away and I was falling into a rhythm of creating and thinking through my hands rather than in my head. I felt comfortable and calm – I didn’t need to think of what I was doing because I’m familiar with this activity, and I can just create while letting my mind just go completely blank. After just a few minutes into painting, I almost forgot I was even feeling anxious at the start, which is something I have never experienced before.

I believe that familiarity with an activity has an influence on how it impacts my mental health. Digital painting is one of my – if not the – favourite forms of creating. I am so comfortable with the activity I can almost go into autopilot and create an artwork without thinking through everything I do. After completing an artwork, my anxieties were almost completely gone, and my stress had decreased significantly. On top of this, my focus and attention increased, making me feel better about going on with the rest of my day. The level to which this craft has assisted in calming my anxiety is almost astounding. I have tried meditation, exercise, relaxation and so much more to help manage my symptoms, and yet I felt like this was one of the best methods.
