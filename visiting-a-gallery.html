My Personal Experience

When I was getting ready to leave for the art gallery, I almost stayed home. My anxiety likes to stay within its bubble, away from people who I don’t know or don’t understand. I felt almost as if lying on the couch and scrolling through art on Instagram would be the same as visiting in person. This will be the first time I visit an art gallery after the COVID lockdowns, and that is also making me a bit uneasy. A few weeks ago, I went to go to the NGV Triennial and turned around to go home after I saw the massive crowd waiting outside, so I’m worried the gallery will be busy.

The gallery was empty. I went to the NGV Australia to just walk around and look – I love art history, so I love just walking through the gallery (though I’ve seen all the works a million times) and getting lost. And, despite everything, this happened again. I got lost in the art. I just put my music on through headphones and strolled – always finding a new artwork I love or appreciating something new about an old one. It felt like I was in a romantic movie. Soft music playing, the girl oblivious to the world around her, completely and uttering engrossed in the art, not caring at all about what was happening around her.

I always forget how impactful walking around a gallery is to me. It seems so simple on the outside, but it is so overwhelmingly peaceful. Walking around a gallery alone, just looking without having to talk or discuss anything is powerful. You can think about and/or just gaze at the works and get lost in everything. I feel like you begin to feel like you’re in some of the artworks, helping sheer sheep or carrying an umbrella through an overflowing market in a big, poufy dress on a hot summer day. I believe it is truly underappreciated how impactful walking through a gallery is – it almost completely eliminated my anxiety and I walked out feeling like both myself and someone new who knew more about the world around her and about the history of the land she lives.
