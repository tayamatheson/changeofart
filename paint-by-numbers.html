My Personal Experience

Similar to traditional painting, I have high hopes that paint by numbers will have a positive impact on my mental health – the fact I don’t have to think about the final product because it’s already chosen for me is exciting, I simply have to paint.

There is something about having 24 different colours of paint on my hands and clothes that I find so incredibly enjoyable. I know so many people that hate this, but it makes me so happy. The act of painting inside of the lines seemed to be helping my mind calm, as I was focused on following the lines instead of the million thoughts racing around in my head.

This activity was quite possibly my favourite for combating my anxiety. I have never gone into an activity feeling so anxious and come out feeling so completely refreshed. The biggest positive is the way this activity assisted my physical anxiety symptoms, specifically shaking. Often, when my anxiety gets bad, I will start to shake like I am cold, when I am not. This activity helped me steady my hands because I was so focused on staying inside the lines, and if my hands shook, I would make a mistake. I think this is a creative activity that would work well for someone who suffers anxiety, like me. Often, unlike some other mental illnesses, you need to calm down before confronting your through when you’re overcome by anxiety. This creative activity allows you to do just that – it calms you down to a point where not only can you paint inside the lines, but you can think inside the lines.
